Speciation, Popgen, Sex Chromosomes

Research

I'm a population geneticist who's interested in the genetic underpinnings of speciation and the evolution of how the genome is organized (in particular sex chromosomes and inversions.) While most of my work is theory driven, I think one of the most exciting aspects of the age of genomic data is that we can at last use real data to parameterize and test our models. Currently, I am studying broad patterns of speciation across groups in Daniel Matute's lab at UNC Chapel Hill as a postdoctoral researcher.
